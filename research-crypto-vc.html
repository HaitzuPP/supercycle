Investment Opportunity · Venture Capital

Most DePIN projects have weak adoption and no durable advantage over centralized alternatives. Early-stage protocol infrastructure with genuine structural advantages is a different category.

Multicoin Capital built $5.9B in AUM by making concentrated bets on protocol infrastructure before the market understood it. The playbook is specific, repeatable, and most allocators are still not running it.

The thesis

Why the playbook works

The DePIN category suffers from selection bias. Most projects have weak product-market fit, speculative token economics, and no path to sustainable adoption beyond the initial community. Multicoin's position is that most of the category is uninvestable: the filter criteria are adoption rate, developer activity, and structural advantages over centralized alternatives. Not market cap. Not token price.

The repeatable Multicoin playbook: find a protocol with a structural advantage over the centralized incumbent (Solana's throughput versus Ethereum's fees, for example), size a meaningful position before institutional capital can move, and hold through the development cycle. Early Solana was a seed-stage entry in 2018 at $0.04 per token. The template has produced repeatable results across multiple cycles.

The harder insight from the Multicoin conversation: being early to infrastructure is a risk category that most allocators do not have the framework to evaluate. The failure mode is picking the wrong protocol: betting on a chain that gets outcompeted before it achieves critical developer mass. The skill is in identifying which infrastructure is genuinely structural versus which is temporarily popular. Developer ecosystem is the leading indicator. Where developers build, users follow.

The opportunity

Where to look

01 · Protocol infrastructure before institutional pricing

The window to enter AI infrastructure protocols (Bittensor and others) at pre-institutional prices is closing. The same window existed for Solana in 2018-2019 and closed once FTX and a16z established positions. The indicators: growing developer activity, increasing subnet registrations, rising validator counts. These are leading metrics that precede price discovery by 12-18 months.

02 · Developer ecosystem as the leading indicator

Track active GitHub commits, SDK downloads, and hackathon participation across competing protocols. These metrics consistently lead price by 12-18 months. A protocol gaining developer share before it gains market share is the clearest early signal in the Multicoin playbook. It is public data, and most allocators are not looking at it.

03 · Structural advantage filter

Any DePIN project worth evaluating needs a specific answer to the question: why can't AWS or Google just do this? If the answer is token economics alone, it fails the filter. If the answer is data sovereignty, censorship resistance, or incentive alignment that a corporation structurally cannot replicate, it passes. Apply this filter before anything else. Most projects fail it in the first conversation.

04 · Specialist fund exposure

Rather than picking individual protocols, backing specialist funds with demonstrated track records and genuine technical diligence is the de-risked version of the same bet. The skill in crypto VC is protocol selection, and it is genuinely scarce. Multicoin, Paradigm, and a handful of others have shown the ability to repeat it. Allocating to that capability is a separate and valid investment decision.
