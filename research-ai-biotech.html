The thesis

Why this is investable now

The drug discovery process has been structurally unchanged for 50 years: screen millions of compounds, identify candidates, run animal trials, run human trials, file for approval. The bottleneck has always been the screening and candidate identification stages. These are exactly the stages most amenable to AI. AlphaFold2's resolution of 200 million protein structures effectively removed the structural biology bottleneck. The candidate generation problem is next.

The open-competition model offers a different architecture than the $2B centralized lab approach. Rather than employing hundreds of researchers to run the same process internally, Nova runs an open network where contributors compete to submit the best molecule candidates for a given disease target. The winner is paid. The economics are similar to NumerAI in quantitative finance: open competition produces better predictions than any closed team at a fraction of the cost. The question is whether the same principle holds in wet lab validation.

The FDA approval risk is real and it is also the opportunity. No AI-discovered compound has cleared Phase III as of 2025. The first one to do so will shift the entire market's assessment of what AI drug discovery is worth. Investors who wait for that confirmation will pay a material premium over current entry prices. The pipeline activity across Isomorphic, Insilico, Recursion, and Nova suggests that moment is within a 2-3 year window.

The opportunity

Where to look

01 · Pre-validation entry on open-competition platforms

The first AI drug to clear FDA Phase III will re-rate the entire category. Companies with clinical-stage AI-discovered compounds are the direct play on that moment. Nova and comparable open-competition platforms are building toward it now, before institutional capital has priced in the probability. The entry window closes with the first approval announcement.

02 · The open-competition model as infrastructure

The NumerAI analogy is instructive: Numerai's open hedge fund aggregates distributed signal and consistently outperforms closed quant funds. Nova applies the same model to molecule discovery. The infrastructure bet is: which platform becomes the default open network for AI drug discovery? Whoever sets that standard captures ongoing value from every candidate submitted to the network.

03 · Tooling and infrastructure picks

The picks-and-shovels layer: molecule generation models, protein structure prediction tooling, and automated wet lab integration. AlphaFold2 created the structural biology infrastructure. The next layer is automated experimental validation, connecting computational prediction to physical confirmation without human-in-the-loop at each step. Companies building that bridge are infrastructure plays on the entire category.

04 · Portfolio construction for binary risk

FDA approval is binary. A portfolio of 5-10 AI drug discovery companies across different disease targets and different computational approaches is the appropriate risk management structure. No single company should be the entire position. The category exposure is the bet: AI-discovered drugs will reach approval, the question is which company gets there first and whether you hold enough of the category to benefit.
