These are the themes we kept returning to across twelve conversations. They are not predictions. They are patterns that showed up repeatedly when we asked founders and investors to explain their bets in plain terms, with real numbers, on the record.

Each theme has a full research note. What follows here is the summary version: the core argument, the key data points, and a quote or two from the conversation that made the thesis concrete.

01 · Decentralized AI

The major labs have locked up the model layer. The infrastructure beneath it is still open.

$18B+ TAO peak market cap
50+ Active subnets
500k+ TAO to register a subnet
DCG Institutional backing via Yuma

The model layer of AI is consolidating quickly. Three companies are spending more on compute than most governments spend on defense, and they are the ones who will own the foundation models. That race is largely run for this cycle.

The infrastructure underneath them is a different question. Compute at the edges, incentive systems for independent model developers, validation of AI outputs without trusting a single entity: none of this has a clear incumbent. Bittensor is the most developed attempt to build it, with a token mechanism that routes rewards to the best-performing subnets and a registration cost high enough to filter out noise.

"Intelligence is moving toward non-human functions for the first time in history. Centralized AI is structurally captured by the same corporate dynamics that captured cloud computing. The open incentive layer wins because it cannot be captured by any single entity."
DCG / Yuma Group · Ep. 11

The investment case is not that Bittensor replaces OpenAI. It is that the infrastructure layer beneath all large models is underpriced, because the market is still pricing TAO like speculative crypto rather than AI infrastructure. The comparable to early AWS holds: picks-and-shovels on a compute market that has not yet been valued as compute.

02 · Robotics

Robot hardware has fallen from $1M to under $100k in five years. The ownership infrastructure has not been built.

553k Robot installations (IFR 2023)
$675M Figure AI Series B
<$100k General-purpose unit cost

The hardware cost curve and the AI capability curve broke at the same time. Boston Dynamics, Agility, Figure, and 1X are all producing general-purpose robots at prices that make industrial deployment viable. Five years ago the same units cost over $1M. That deflationary pressure is structural, not cyclical: it follows the same pattern as solar panels, LIDAR, and genome sequencing.

The question that follows is not whether robots proliferate. It is who captures the economic upside when they do. Amazon, Tesla, and Foxconn buying robot fleets means the upside accrues to their equity holders. Decentralized robot ownership is a different architecture: productive robot infrastructure that individuals or collectives can own, deploy, and earn from.

"Spatial computing is the operating system for robots in shared environments. Without a shared map, every robot is working blind. The infrastructure needs to exist before the deployment wave, not after."
Auki Labs · Ep. 3

The infrastructure picks: spatial computing networks (shared maps for robots operating in the same environment), on-chain ownership and task coordination protocols, and decentralized robot fleet financing. The market is pricing the humanoid robotics story as a 10-year outcome. The infrastructure enabling that story needs to be built in the next 3 years.

03 · AI Biotech

No AI-discovered drug has cleared the FDA. The first that does re-rates the entire category.

$2B Isomorphic Labs raise
200M+ AlphaFold2 protein structures
12-15yr Traditional discovery timeline
2-3yr AI-compressed target

The drug discovery pipeline has been structurally unchanged for 50 years. Screen millions of compounds, identify candidates, run trials, file for approval. The bottleneck has always been candidate identification, and that is exactly where AI has the clearest advantage. AlphaFold2's resolution of 200 million protein structures effectively removed the structural biology bottleneck. Candidate generation is next.

The binary risk is real and it is also the opportunity. No AI-discovered compound has cleared Phase III. The first one that does will shift the entire market's assessment of what AI drug discovery is worth. Investors who wait for that confirmation will pay a significant premium over current entry prices.

"An open-competition network for molecule candidates produces better predictions than any closed team at a fraction of the cost. The same reason NumerAI's open hedge fund outperforms closed quant funds. Distributed signal beats concentrated effort when the problem is fundamentally one of search."
Nova · AI Drug Discovery

The open-competition model is the structural bet. Rather than spending $2B on internal researchers running the same process, an open network rewards contributors for results rather than effort. The economics are the same reason prediction markets consistently outperform expert panels. The question is whether the same principle holds when the validation moves from digital prediction to wet lab confirmation.

04 · Agent Economy

AI agents generate the majority of internet traffic. The infrastructure was built for browsers.

50%+ Non-human internet traffic (Cloudflare)
$2B+ Agent infrastructure funding (2024)

The shift is not coming. It is underway. Cloudflare's network data shows bot and agent traffic constitutes the majority of HTTP requests across the internet. Most of that traffic hits infrastructure designed for browsers, not programmatic clients. The friction is real and commercial at each layer where it exists.

The analogy to the early mobile internet is instructive. In 2008, web infrastructure was built for desktop browsers. The teams that built mobile-native infrastructure captured outsized value: Stripe for mobile payments, Twilio for mobile messaging. The constraint was not demand. It was the absence of the infrastructure itself.

"Most internet infrastructure assumes a human is making decisions at the other end of every request. That assumption is breaking down. The protocols that survive the transition will be those that assume agents are the primary user, not a special case."
0xSammy · Ep. 1

The infrastructure bets: agent identity (how does a service know which agent is calling, the SSL certificate equivalent), autonomous payment rails (an agent that cannot pay cannot act in the world), and agent-native APIs built for programmatic consumption with structured schemas and machine-readable documentation. The window to set these standards is the next two to three years.

05 · Crypto VC

Most DePIN has no structural advantage over centralized alternatives. Early protocol infrastructure is a different bet.

$5.9B Multicoin AUM
$0.04 Early Solana seed price
12-18mo Developer activity lead time

Multicoin built $5.9B in AUM by making concentrated bets on protocol infrastructure before the market understood it. The Solana position started at $0.04 per token in 2018. The playbook is specific: find a protocol with a structural advantage over the centralized incumbent, size the position before institutions can move, and hold through the development cycle. Being early to the wrong protocol is not the same bet.

The filter Shayon uses for DePIN is direct: does this protocol have a structural advantage over the centralized alternative, or is it decentralization for its own sake? Most DePIN fails that test. The failure modes are speculative token economics, no path to adoption beyond the initial community, and no technical moat once a well-funded centralized competitor decides to compete. Solana passed the test because the throughput advantage over Ethereum was real and measurable before the market priced it.

"Developer ecosystem is the leading indicator. Track GitHub commits and hackathon participation. Where developers build is where users follow, consistently, by 12 to 18 months."
Shayon Sengupta, Multicoin Capital · Ep. 4

The current window: AI infrastructure protocols, including Bittensor and comparable networks, are at a comparable stage to Solana in 2018 to 2019. Growing developer activity, increasing subnet registrations, and rising validator counts are the leading metrics. These precede price discovery. Institutions have not established meaningful positions yet. The window closes with the first major institutional announcement.
